Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ) medication

Comprehensive Drug Profile for Hydrochlorothiazide

history_edu Introduction

History & Drug Category

Hydrochlorothiazide (HCTZ) is a thiazide diuretic developed in the 1950s, widely used for treating hypertension and edema.

Class: Thiazide diuretic
Brand Name: Diuza

Chemical Formula: 6-chloro-3,4-dihydro-2H-1,2,4-benzothiadiazine-7-sulfonamide 1,1-dioxide
Molecular Formula: C₇H₈ClN₃O₄S₂
Molecular Weight: 297.75 g/mol

Stable under recommended storage conditions.

science Pharmacology & Mechanism of Action

Hydrochlorothiazide inhibits the sodium-chloride symporter in the distal convoluted tubule of the nephron, increasing sodium, chloride, and water excretion. This leads to decreased plasma volume and reduced blood pressure.

local_hospital Indications

A. Hypertension

Hydrochlorothiazide is indicated as first line in the management of hypertension either alone or in combination with other antihypertensive drugs in severe conditions.

B. Edema

Hydrochlorothiazide is used as adjunctive therapy in edema associated with:

  • Congestive heart failure
  • Hepatic cirrhosis
  • Corticosteroid and estrogen therapy
  • Various forms of renal dysfunction such as nephrotic syndrome, acute glomerulonephritis, and chronic renal failure

C. Off-label use

It can also be used to treat nephrogenic diabetes insipidus and prevent calcium nephrolithiasis.

biotech Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Absorption

Well absorbed with a bioavailability of 65-75%. Absorption may be reduced in congestive heart failure patients.

Distribution

Crosses the placenta but not the blood-brain barrier; excreted in breast milk. Volume of distribution is approximately 3.6 - 7.8 L/kg. Protein binding ranges 40-68%.

Metabolism & Elimination

Not metabolized. Eliminated rapidly by kidneys. At least 61% of an oral dose is excreted unchanged in urine within 24 hours.

schedule Dosage

medication Administration Guidelines

💊 Oral Use

  • Take with or without food.
  • Food may delay absorption slightly — not clinically significant.

⏰ Timing

  • Once daily, preferably in the morning to avoid nighttime urination.
  • In some edema cases, dosing may be split.

⚠️ Special Considerations

  • Renal impairment: Adjust dose & monitor closely.
  • Follow local clinical guidelines — regimens may vary (e.g., Pakistan vs. global practices).

warning Adverse Effects

block Contraindications

  • Anuria (inability to produce urine).
  • Hypersensitivity to hydrochlorothiazide or sulfonamides.
  • Renal decompensation.

warning Warnings and Precautions

  • Use cautiously in severe renal disease, gout, diabetes, and high cholesterol.
  • Thiazides may worsen kidney function and cause azotemia; use carefully in liver disease due to risk of hepatic coma.
  • Avoid use with lithium.

monitoring Monitoring Parameters

  • 💧 Electrolytes: Sodium, potassium, magnesium, calcium.
  • 🩸 Kidney Health: Serum creatinine, BUN.
  • 💓 Blood Pressure: Regular check-ups.
  • ⚖️ Metabolic: Glucose, lipids.

family_restroom Special Population

  • Pregnancy: Category B; use only if benefits justify risks.
  • Lactation: Excreted in breast milk; caution advised.
  • Geriatrics: Increased sensitivity; monitor renal function closely.

medical_services Toxicity

May increase photosensitivity and UV-associated phototoxicity risks.
